The Full Breakdown

Fees: Remitly wins — not even close

Remitly charges between $0 and $3.99 depending on your payment method and whether you have an active promotion. Western Union charges $8.99 for the same $300 transfer.

That's already up to $6 more just on the fee. And that's only half the story.

Exchange rate: Remitly wins here too

Western Union doesn't just charge more — it also gives fewer lempiras per dollar. Remitly offers 26.55 HNL per USD; Western Union offers 26.10. The 0.45 HNL/USD gap sounds small, but on $300:

  • 300 × 26.55 = 7,965 HNL (Remitly, before fee)
  • 300 × 26.10 = 7,830 HNL (Western Union, before fee)

Then Western Union charges $8.99 on top of that. Your family ends up receiving 207 fewer lempiras with Western Union.

Speed: both are fast, but Remitly is more consistent

Remitly delivers in minutes to Honduran bank accounts (Bancatlán, Atlántida, BAC, Ficohsa, and more). Western Union can also be fast — 2 hours to 1 day — but it varies more depending on the method. If urgency is the key factor, Remitly is the more reliable bet.

Cash vs. bank transfer: this is where Western Union has a real edge

Western Union has a massive network of agents and cash pickup locations across Honduras, including rural areas where there's no nearby bank branch. If your family doesn't have a bank account, or they live somewhere far from banking, Western Union may be the only practical option. That matters — and it's real.

When to use Remitly

Use Remitly if:

  • Your family has a bank account in Honduras
  • You want the best price possible
  • You want money to arrive in minutes
  • You send frequently — the savings add up fast

When to use Western Union

Use Western Union if:

  • Your family needs to pick up cash
  • They live in an area without banking access
  • An elderly family member doesn't have an account or isn't comfortable with digital transfers
  • It's an emergency and you know there's an agent nearby

What about promotions?

Remitly frequently offers $0 fee on your first transfer. If you've never used it, that first send can be literally free. Western Union also has occasional deals, but they're less consistent. If you're trying a new service, start with Remitly.

What nobody tells you about Western Union

Western Union has been around for decades, and many families trust it for exactly that reason. That trust is earned. But knowing how much more it costs gives you the power to decide. If your family can receive bank transfers, you're probably overpaying every month — and you don't have to be.

The Bottom Line

  • For price: Remitly wins in almost every case — lower fees, better rate, more lempiras for your family.
  • For cash in remote areas: Western Union is still hard to beat.
  • For urgency: Both are fast, but Remitly is more consistent.

If you send regularly to a family with a bank account, switching to Remitly could save your family more than 2,400 HNL per year.
