The Two Costs Nobody Explains Well

When you send money to Honduras, there are two ways the service charges you:

1. The visible fee

This is what you can clearly see: "$3.99 fee" or "$8.99 charge." Easy to compare.

2. The exchange rate margin (the hidden cost)

This is where the real money goes. The interbank market exchange rate in April 2026 is around 26.70 HNL per dollar (you can verify this at the Banco Central de Honduras or on XE.com). No service gives you that rate — they all adjust it downward and pocket the difference.

For example:

  • Remitly gives you 26.55 (keeps 0.15 HNL/USD)
  • Western Union gives you 26.10 (keeps 0.60 HNL/USD)

On $200, that rate difference alone equals 90 fewer lempiras with Western Union — before even counting the fee.

Why Do Rates Vary So Much Between Services?

Each company negotiates differently with banks and payment networks in Honduras. Some have more favorable agreements; others compensate for lower fees with a worse exchange rate.

Rates change every day — sometimes twice a day. A service that was cheapest on Tuesday might not be on Thursday. That's why comparing before every transfer makes real sense. It's not paranoia. It's just math.

Golden rule: always pay with a bank account or debit card.
